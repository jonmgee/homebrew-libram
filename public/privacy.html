Privacy

Homebrew Libram & PC on Parchment · last updated 20 July 2026

The short version

One account covers both sites. We store your email address and whatever you create — homebrew, characters and any images you upload. We don't track you, there's no analytics and no advertising.

Two things worth knowing up front: images you upload are stored in a public place, so anyone with the direct link can view them; and if you use the AI import, the content you submit is sent to an AI company to be read. Both are explained below.

You can delete your account and everything in it, yourself, instantly, from the Account page. It really does delete it all.

Who we are

Homebrew Libram and PC on Parchment are run by Appwrights Guild, a small hobby project based in the United Kingdom. For data protection purposes, Appwrights Guild is the data controller. You can reach us at appwrightsguild@proton.me.

The two sites share one account system and one database. An account made on either works on both, and anything that happens to your account — a password change, or deletion — applies to both.

What we store

Your account

  • Your email address.
  • Your password, stored only as a cryptographic hash. We cannot read it, and neither can anyone else.
  • The dates you signed up, last signed in, and confirmed your email.

What you create

  • Homebrew entries — names, descriptions, stat blocks, tags, campaign names and anything else you type in.
  • Characters — the whole sheet, including free-text boxes like backstory, appearance and personality. Whatever you write there, we store.
  • Images you upload for entry artwork or character portraits.
  • Share links you create.

These are meant for game content, but they're free-text boxes — if you type personal details into a character's backstory, that gets stored too. Worth bearing in mind.

How often you use the AI import

We record the date and time of each AI import you run, against your account. This is only used to enforce a daily limit, so one account can't run up a large bill. It is not used to build any profile of you.

Uploaded images are publicly accessible

This one deserves its own heading. Images you upload are stored in a public location, so anyone who has the direct link to an image can view it without signing in.

The links contain a long random identifier, so they can't realistically be guessed or listed, and nothing points to them unless you share an entry. But they are not protected by your password. Please don't upload anything you'd be unhappy for a stranger to see if the link got out.

When you delete your account, your uploaded images are deleted too, and the links stop working immediately.

The AI import

Both sites can read homebrew or a character sheet using AI. This only happens when you choose to use it.

When you do, the text or image you submit is sent to OpenRouter, which passes it to Google's Gemini model to be read and turned into structured data. We don't control what those companies do with it, and their terms apply once it reaches them. If that's not something you want, simply don't use the import feature — everything can be typed in by hand instead.

Copying an entry from a shared libram does not use AI. That's a straight copy inside our own database, and nothing leaves it.

Sharing

If you create a share link, anything covered by that link becomes readable by anyone who has it, without signing in. That's the point of it, but it's worth being deliberate — a link that gets forwarded is a link that works. Entries you've marked DM-only are excluded from shared librams and never sent to the browser.

Someone viewing your shared libram can copy entries into their own. Once they do, that copy belongs to them, and it stays in their account even if you later delete yours. Those copies carry the content only — nothing identifying you travels with them. Revoking a share link, or deleting your account, kills the link immediately but does not reach into other people's collections.

How long we keep it

Until you delete it. There's no automatic expiry: your homebrew and characters stay put until you remove them or delete your account.

When you delete your account, everything goes immediately and permanently — the account, your entries, your characters, your share links, your usage records and your uploaded images, across both sites. There's no grace period, no archive and no backup copy kept afterwards. We can't undo it, so please be sure.

Why we're allowed to store it

Our lawful basis is performance of a contract. You asked us to keep your homebrew and characters, and we can't do that without storing them. The same applies to the AI import, which runs only when you choose to use it.

Who else touches it

  • Vercel hosts the websites.
  • Supabase hosts the database, the login system and the image storage.
  • OpenRouter and Google receive whatever you submit to the AI import, and only that.

Vercel and Supabase keep their own server logs, which include your IP address as a normal part of serving web traffic. We don't use those logs to profile you or combine them with anything else.

We do not sell your data, share it for advertising, or pass it to anyone else.

Cookies and tracking

There are none. No analytics, no advertising, no tracking pixels, no third-party fonts. The only thing kept in your browser is the token that keeps you signed in, which is required for the site to work at all and isn't used for anything else.

Your rights

Under UK data protection law you can:

  • See your data — most of it is on screen whenever you're signed in.
  • Correct it — edit anything, any time.
  • Delete it — the Account page does this yourself, instantly, with no need to ask us.
  • Get a copy — we haven't built an export button yet. Email us and we'll send you your data.
  • Object to how we use it, or ask us to restrict it.

Changing the email address on an account isn't something you can do yourself yet — email us and we'll sort it.

Children

These sites need an account, so they're intended for users aged 13 or over. They collect no personal details beyond an email address, and we'd suggest younger players avoid putting real names or personal details into character sheets — good practice at the table anyway.

Changes

If we change what we collect or who we send it to, we'll update this page and the date at the top.

Complaints

If you think we've handled your data badly, please tell us first at appwrightsguild@proton.me and we'll try to put it right. You also have the right to complain to the Information Commissioner's Office, the UK's data protection regulator, at ico.org.uk.
